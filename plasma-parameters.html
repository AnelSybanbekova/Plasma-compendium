Plasma Parameters

The main properties of plasma are quasi-neutrality, electrical conductivity, collective particle behavior, Debye shielding, and plasma oscillations.

Quasi-Neutrality

Plasma is generally electrically neutral because the number of electrons is approximately equal to the number of positive ions:

ne ≈ ni

Local charge imbalances can occur and create electric fields and currents, but on a large scale plasma remains electrically neutral.

Electrical Conductivity

Because plasma consists of charged particles, it has high electrical conductivity and strongly interacts with electromagnetic fields. Therefore, plasma can be controlled using electric and magnetic fields.

Collective Behavior

Particles in plasma interact through electric and magnetic fields. Unlike neutral gases, where interactions mainly occur through collisions, plasma demonstrates collective effects such as plasma waves, turbulence, and instabilities.

Debye Shielding

Debye shielding is the phenomenon in which charged particles rearrange around an introduced charge and weaken its electric field.

The characteristic distance is called the Debye length λD. At distances larger than λD, the original electric field becomes strongly reduced.

Plasma Oscillations

Plasma oscillations are collective oscillations of electrons relative to ions.

ωp = √((nee²)/(ε0me))

where:

  • ne – electron density;
  • e – electron charge;
  • me – electron mass;
  • ε0 – vacuum permittivity.

Cold and Hot Plasma

Hot Plasma

Hot plasma is plasma where the temperatures of electrons, ions, and neutral particles are approximately equal:

Te ≈ Ti ≈ Tn

Such plasma reaches temperatures of millions of kelvin. Fusion reactors operate at approximately 107–108 K.

Cold Plasma

Cold plasma has a much higher electron temperature compared with ions and neutral particles:

Te ≫ Ti ≈ Tn

Electrons heat quickly because of their small mass, while ions remain close to room temperature (~300 K).

Cold plasma is used in plasma medicine, plasma lamps, surface treatment, and semiconductor technologies.
