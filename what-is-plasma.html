What is Plasma?

Plasma is an ionized gas consisting of free electrons and positive and negative ions. It is often referred to as the fourth state of matter.

Plasma research is important primarily in energy and astrophysics. Plasma is used to create thermonuclear fusion, a reaction occurring in the Sun:

D + T → He + n + 17.6 MeV

One gram of a deuterium–tritium mixture can give 3.4 × 1011 J of energy. For comparison, 1 gram of oil gives 4.2 × 104 J.

The world's fuel reserves are depleted every day:

  • Oil: approximately 70 years
  • Natural gas: approximately 50 years
  • Coal: approximately 125 years

Therefore, new types of energy are needed besides atomic energy.

Plasma in Astrophysics

Stars, the solar wind and the interstellar medium are filled with plasma. Therefore, astrophysics studies plasma as well.

How Plasma is Created

Plasma can be obtained from almost any material if enough energy is transferred. Gases are usually the most suitable materials:

  • Argon
  • Neon
  • Xenon
  • Hydrogen
  • Helium
  • Nitrogen
  • Air

Methods of Plasma Creation

Heating

The gas is heated. The kinetic and potential energy of atoms increases. Electrons leave atoms and become free electrons, while atoms become ions. Plasma is formed.

Electric Discharge

A strong electric current passes through gas. The electric field accelerates electrons. They collide with atoms, remove electrons and ionization occurs.

Laser Plasma Creation

A very powerful laser transfers energy to matter. Electrons become free and ionization occurs. Plasma is created.

Microwave Plasma Creation

An electromagnetic field accelerates electrons. They collide with atoms, creating ions and forming plasma.
